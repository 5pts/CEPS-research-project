## Slide 3

Research Question

Research Question: Can school social capital compensate for family disadvantage (SES & Hukou)?
Hypotheses:
H1 (Main Effect): Both Teacher Bonding and Peer Bridging positively predict educational expectations.
H2 (Compensatory Effect):
H2a: Teacher Bonding benefits Low-SES students > High-SES students.
H2b: Peer Bridging benefits Low-SES students > High-SES students.
H2c: Teacher Bonding benefits Rural Hukou students > Urban Hukou students.
H3 (Exploratory): Comparing the magnitude: Which compensatory effect is stronger? (Bonding vs. Bridging).

---

## Slide 4

Research Question

Two paths to educational expectations:
Peer bonding → "getting by" (emotional support)
Teacher bridging → "getting ahead" (institutional resources)
Key question: Does being a migrant change these effects?
Migrants may benefit less from peer ties (isolation in migrant cliques)
Migrants may benefit more from teacher ties (compensates for family disadvantage)
